Pani Puri

Coming from one of the most populous countries in the world, Indian food reflects the unique blend of cultures and regional identities found throughout the country. Shaped by its long and tumultuous history, Indian cuisine carries influences of colonizers from the East and the West as well as a range of religious beliefs and practices.

INGREDIENTS

    For the puri:
  1. Semolina flour
  2. Wheat flour
  3. Water
  4. Oil
    For the pani (water):
  1. Mint leaves
  2. Coriander leaves
  3. Green chilies
  4. Tamarind paste
  5. Sugar
  6. Black salt
  7. Water

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Make the dough for the puri by mixing semolina flour, wheat flour, and water. Roll out thin circles and fry them in hot oil until puffed.
  2. Blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, tamarind paste, sugar, black salt, and cumin powder with water to make the pani. Adjust the consistency and taste as desired.
  3. Mash the boiled potatoes and chickpeas together.
  4. Assemble the pani puri by filling each puri with a little potato mixture, green chutney, tamarind chutney, chopped onion, and sev. Pour some pani into the puri and enjoy!

TIPS

  1. You can buy readymade puris from Indian grocery stores
  2. Adjust the spice level of the pani and filling to your preference.
  3. Serve pani puri immediately as the puris become soggy over time.
